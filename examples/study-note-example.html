Aletheia: truth as unconcealment

A one-page distillation you might generate from your second brain — then open in ALETHEIA to annotate, restructure and mark up before a review session.

The core idea

For Heidegger, truth (aletheia, ἀλήθεια) is not first a property of statements but an event of unconcealment: beings come out of hiddenness into the open. The Greek is privative — a- (not) + lethe (concealment) — literally "un-forgetting".

The essence of truth is the truth of essence: the clearing in which beings show themselves.

Key terms

  • Aletheia — unconcealment; truth as disclosure rather than correspondence.
  • Lichtung — the "clearing", the open region where things become present and visible.
  • Lethe — concealment / forgetting; the ground against which disclosure happens.

Why it matters for study

  1. It reframes "knowing" as making visible, not just matching labels.
  2. It connects to perception, attention and what a tool lets you see.
  3. It is the root metaphor behind this very editor's name.
